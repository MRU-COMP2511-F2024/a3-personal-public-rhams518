Family

My family consists of my parents, Angela and Lloyd, and my brother, Ethan. I’m the youngest in my family by eight years. Despite the age gap between me and my brother, we get along really well—likely because we never had to compete for the same spaces or attention while growing up. For Father's Day, we got my dad a personalized Simpsons-style family portrait, which he absolutely loved.

I still live with my parents and plan to stay with them until I graduate. My brother, however, lives in California and works for Apple. Even though he’s far away, he always comes home for Christmas. I’m particularly excited to see him this year when he flies in on December 15th—it’s always great to have the whole family together.

Dance

I used to dance from the time I was three years old until I turned 18. Spending so many years dancing taught me invaluable lessons about hard work, discipline, and collaboration—skills I’m incredibly grateful for learning at such a young age. Recently, I’ve found myself missing it a lot, and I’m hoping to find an adult dance class to reignite my passion. One of the coolest experiences I’ve had was traveling to New York City with my friends to dance in Times Square! It was a surreal moment that I’ll cherish forever.

Dogs

Now, onto my dogs—Amy and Pico. Amy, the black dog, is an eight-year-old Collie/Shepherd mix. She was originally meant to be my dog, but she’s completely obsessed with my dad, so she’s unofficially his. Amy is the sweetest, smartest dog you could meet, and she thrives on attention from people.

Pico, on the other hand, is my one-year-old ginger-colored Husky/Shepherd mix, and she fully embraces being my dog. Unlike Amy, Pico is wonderfully goofy and not the brightest, but her silly antics make her absolutely endearing. I wouldn’t have her any other way. Together, Amy and Pico bring so much joy and love into our lives—they’re truly part of the family.
